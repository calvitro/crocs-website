Four graduate students in the CROCS Lab were awarded grants from the Student Conference Travel Fund for presentations at the American Society of Criminology conference in Washington, DC.

<!--more--> 

<a href=/team/isaac-moelter>Isaac Moelter</a>, <a href=/team/alice-jurkiewicz>Alice Jurkiewicz</a>, <a href=/team/sarah-schappert>Sarah Schappert</a>, and <a href=/team/callie-vitro>Callie Vitro</a> each received grants from the UNO Student Conference Travel Fund to present their research at ASC in Washington, DC. 

{{< notice "Presentations" >}}
**“Support our POWs”: An online forum for white supremacists behind bars** *Isaac Moelter* 
<br><br>
**Awareness of and attitudes toward anti-Asian hate crimes post COVID-19** *Alice Jurkiewicz* & Erin     Kearns
<br><br>
**Content analysis of K-12 Behavioral Threat Assessment and Management (BTAM) guides: Implementing responses to exogenous shocks** *Sarah Schappert*
<br><br>
**Breaking the silence: How anonymity, reporting methods, and authorities impact willingness to report suspicious activity** *Callie Vitro*, Isaac Moelter, & Erin Kearns
